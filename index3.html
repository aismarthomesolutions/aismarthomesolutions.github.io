The Possibilities

What can a smart home do for you?

Lighting

Want to control all your lights with a single command? Want random lights to turn on & off when you're away? No more running around the house to turn everything off.

Heating & Cooling

Adjust the thermostat from anywhere in the world whether you're at work or on vacation so it's always the perfect temperature when you're home; and saving you money when you're not.

Security

Hate having to look for the keys every time you go out? Don't worry, your door will unlock as you're pulling into the driveway.

Home Entertainment

Was it HDMI 2 or 5? Gone are the days of having a separate remote for everything. One click and your TV and sound system will turn on to the exact channel or movie you want.

Voice Control

No need to walk up to the light switch or the thermostat anymore. Just say what you want.

Sensors

Sensors: the backbone of automation. Know exactly when you & your family came & went. Know when something opens or closes. Know when there's people over when there shouldn't be.

Piecing it all together

Instead of focusing on just the lights, or just the thermostat; we offer the full package. After all, your home isn’t just made up of a couple lights; its home because of the way everything comes together.

Check out a sample of what is possible below.

GOOD MORNING

Every morning, it’s the same thing…

  • You snooze your alarm 11 times until you’re 30 minutes behind schedule and have no choice but to get up…
  • …except it’s the middle of the winter and the last thing you want to do is leave the warmth of your blanket.
  • Another 30 minutes go by before you muster the courage to make a dash for the thermostat
  • Lastly, you plug in the iron and pray it heats up in time.

What a great start to the day...

Imagine instead…

  • 30 minutes before your alarm goes off, the heat comes on so it’s the perfect temperature to ease you out of bed
  • 15 minutes before your alarm goes off, the iron turns on so its nice and hot when you need it
  • It’s time to get up and your favorite tunes start playing to get your day going

You actually have time for breakfast for once
…all this happened without you raising a finger

GOOD BYE

YOU FINALLY MADE IT TO DINNER

  • It would have been nice to turn off the lights before heading out but you showed up an hour late as it is.
  • Is the hair straightener still plugged in? Please don’t burn the house down.
  • Hopefully you at least remembered to lock the door since you didn’t recognize those two guys outside.

It’s going to be hard to enjoy your night out with all these things on your mind.

Imagine instead…

As you’re pulling out the neighborhood

  • Everything that doesn’t need to be on is turned off
  • Your door double checks to make sure its locked (just in case you forgot), and if there are any curious individuals also checking, it’ll let you know.

All this happened automatically. No running around the house. Didn't even have to open an app.

VACATION

EVERYONE KNOWS THE TRICK: TURN ON THE TV AND LIGHTS BEFORE GOING ON YOUR WEEK LONG VACATION

  • It makes your home seem occupied 24/7, which also means your electronics are on 24/7.
  • That’s odd, the same lights are on for the past four days, even during the day.
  • Don’t want your home to become a fridge? Better leave the heat on the whole time as well.

Who knew utilities cost even more when you’re not using it?

Imagine instead…

  • Landing in paradise without having to decide between break-ins and draining the power grid because you know your lights will turn on & off at random times (but only when its dark out). Someone is definitely home!
  • Still worried? Feel free to check-in while waiting in line at any of the tourist attractions wherever they may be.

Oh did we mention, the heat turned down when you left for the week to save energy but readjusted just in time to make sure your home is nice and warm when you get back?

MOVIE NIGHT

It's movie night!

  • Where the remote go this time?
  • Which input was the Blu-ray again?
  • What about for surround sound on the receiver?

After flipping the house upside down and cycling through 34 different inputs, everything is finally perfect. You snuggle up and press play.

Shoot, you forgot the lights.

Imagine instead…

  • You let your remotes hangout in the underworld of seat cushions and just worry about finding the perfect position on the couch.
  • When you’re ready, just say “it’s movie night”.
  • The TV flips to HDMI 3, the receiver goes to Video 2, and the lights dim down to 5% brightness.

It’s almost as if you’re at the movie theater, minus the overpriced popcorn and the noisy couple sitting two seats over.
